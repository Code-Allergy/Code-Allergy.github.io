CMPT370 Project Proposal

Overview

We are a team of students from the University of Saskatchewan (USask) undertaking a software development project as part of our class assignment. Our goal is to develop a free software product over a period of approximately 10 weeks. This proposal outlines the project details, objectives, and expectations.

Team Composition

  • Team Size: 5 students
  • Affiliation: University of Saskatchewan, Department of Computer Science
  • Roles: Each team member will assume different roles, including project management, software development, quality assurance, and documentation.

Project Details

  • Development Period: Approximately 10 weeks
  • Product Delivery: The software will be provided free of charge.
  • Support: The product will be delivered as-is, with no guarantee of ongoing support or maintenance.
  • Scope: The final product may be unfinished or significantly scoped down depending on the progress and challenges encountered during the development period.

Objectives

  1. Design and Development:

    • Develop a functional and user-friendly software product.
    • Ensure that the software adheres to open-source principles and best practices.

  2. Documentation:

    • Create comprehensive documentation for users and developers, including installation guides, user manuals, and API documentation.

  3. Testing and Quality Assurance:

    • Conduct thorough testing to identify and resolve bugs and issues.
    • Ensure the software meets the agreed-upon specifications and performance criteria.

  4. Stakeholder Engagement:

    • Maintain regular communication with stakeholders about project status and necessary changes.
    • Schedule biweekly meetings to demonstrate progress and solicit feedback. (This is optional, but we would really like to meet with you!)

Project Scope

  • Limitations: We are open to most ideas, however we do have to place a few restrictions:
    • Platform Restrictions:
      • We will not work on products exclusively for the Apple ecosystem (e.g., iOS, macOS) due to restrictive development practices and limited cross-platform compatibility.
    • Existing Solutions:
      • We will not work on products that already have a well-established free and open-source alternative. The goal is to provide unique value or address a niche that is not adequately covered by existing solutions.
    • Complexity:
      • We will avoid highly complex projects that require extensive development beyond the 10-week timeframe. Projects should be feasible within the allocated period, focusing on a specific, manageable set of features.
    • Legal and Compliance Issues:
      • We will not engage in projects that involve handling sensitive personal data or require compliance with complex legal or regulatory standards, such as GDPR or HIPAA, due to the limited timeframe and expertise.
    • Resource Constraints:
      • We will avoid projects requiring advanced hardware or specific infrastructure that we do not have access to. The project should be achievable with standard development tools and resources available to a student team.
    • Technology Stack:
      • We will use widely supported and well-documented technologies to ensure the development process is manageable and the final product is maintainable by others. Avoiding obscure or experimental technologies will help mitigate risks associated with technical difficulties.

By adhering to these restrictions, we aim to ensure that our project is feasible, valuable, and aligned with the capabilities and resources of our student team.

Example Project

Last year, my previous team developed a solution for a local car dealership to streamline their operations. We transitioned their complex and unwieldy spreadsheet system to a user-friendly online platform. Sales representatives could input their data into an intuitive form, while administrators gained access to comprehensive views of the data and additional statistics.

Due to unforeseen team challenges, the project was not fully completed. As a result, you might encounter some issues when browsing the site.

Deliverables

  1. Software Product: A working version of the software that meets the defined objectives and features.
  2. Documentation: Complete and user-friendly documentation.
  3. Source Code: All source code will be made available under an open-source license (likely MIT or a more permissive licence).
  4. Project Report: A final report summarizing the development process, challenges faced, and lessons learned. We will also include our presentation given to our class, detailing additional technical information.

Conclusion

This project represents an opportunity to apply our software development skills in a practical setting while contributing to the local community. We acknowledge the potential challenges and limitations but are committed to delivering a valuable product within the given timeframe.
